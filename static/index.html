Passion

Code.^500 Craftsmanship and Hacking.

In my professional life I gain a lot of experience in agile software development, project management and devops. In addition to management and fullstack development of customer projects, my responsibilities include the administration of multiple Kubernetes and Proxmox clusters.
During my bachelor studies, my second passion developed in the field of IT security. When I found myself absorbing various papers about exploits with great fascination, I knew I had to complete my master's degree in this field.
At home, I am gaining supplementary knowledge through the administration of my server as well as my own programming projects.

Electronics.^500 Making and Tweaking.

I use my fascination for technology to tinker, not only with software, but also with additional hardware. As exemplary with this macro keyboard, I used this project to familiarize myself with arduinos during university, utilizing my 3d printer and soldering iron.
Or that time when I didn't want to wait several weeks for delivery and went into a deep rabbit hole - consisting of github code snippets and soldering on the board of a zigbee controller - just to flash an alternative firmware.

Sports.^500 Climbing and Biking.

In sport I find the ideal, but also necessary, balance to the digital.
In bouldering I can challenge myself and constantly progress technically and powerfully, just like I do in front of the screen.
Riding a racing bike, in contrast, is the literal example of moving forward. Perfect to let your mind wander and switch off.
Complementary to this, for deceleration I go on hikes through the woods and in the mountains, as long as I'm in nature.

Music.^500 Listening and Mixing.

Music has always been an important part of my life - the soundtrack, so to speak.
When I'm DJing I can be creative, as soon as the music starts I'm happy.
I am not bound to one genre, my selection ranges from house to reggae to funk to jazz to jungle to hiphop.
